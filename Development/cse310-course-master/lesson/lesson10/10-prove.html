W10 Prove: Practice Interviewing

Reading and Activities

The Self Reliance Services (SRS) “Find a Better Job” manual is intended for use in a group, but you will use it individually. Activities and discussions in the reading should be done individually.

This week, you should complete the following reading:

  • Chapter 6, Section 1—Prepare before the Interview, Page 100

  • In the activity, you will first need to find an entry-level or internship job that you are interested in. You can search on websites like GlassDoor, Indeed, or Handshake. You can find more resources at the BYU-Idaho Career Center.

  • Chapter 6, Section 2—Open the Interview Effectively, Page 101

    • In the activity, do the comparison of the pictures on your own.

  • Chapter 6, Section 3—Use Your Me in 30 Seconds, Pages 101–103

    • In the first activity, do the role-playing on your own.

    • In the second activity, share your Me in 30 Seconds with two or more friends or family members and ask for feedback.

  • Chapter 6, Section 4—Use Power Statements, Pages 103–105 (excluding the third activity)

    • In the first activity, do the role-playing on your own.

    • In the second activity, share your responses with two or more friends or family members and ask for feedback.

  • Chapter 7, Section 2—Ask Questions, Page 121 (excluding the activity)

  • Chapter 7, Section 3—Close the Interview Effectively, Page 122

  • Chapter 7, Conclusion, Page 123

    • You should watch the video Amy Nails Her Interview referenced in this section.

Assignment

During the reading from the SRS “Finding a Better Job” manual, you completed activities that anticipated your responses to interview questions. Your assignment is to provide well-thought-out answers to these questions.

  1. Download the template cse310_interviewing.docx.

  2. Answer the following question using a power statement: “How well do you work with others in a team?”

  3. Answer the following question using a power statement: “What experience do you have in developing software?” (NOTE: If you are not studying to be a software developer, then change this question to be appropriate for your major.)

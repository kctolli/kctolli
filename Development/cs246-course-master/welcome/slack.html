Objectives

  • Understand what Slack is.

  • Understand how to connect to Slack.

  • Understand Slack etiquette.

Slack is a team communication service that we'll be using for this class. It's like a chat room, but with a lot of extra features such as file-sharing, threaded-discussions, and search capabilities that make it ideal for software teams. Like other tools we'll use in this class, Slack is widely used in the software industry.

Depending on how your instructor sets up Slack for your course, you'll either receive a Slack invitation via email, or you will be provided with a signup URL by the instructor. Either way, click the provided link and follow the directions to get signed up. Afterwards, you can download a Slack client for your computer or mobile device if you wish, or continue to use the web interface.

Slack Channels

Communication in Slack is divided into channels. Most of our class-wide communication will happen in the #general channel.

Once project teams have been established, private team channels will be created for each team to use.

In addition, you can send direct messages to your instructor (or to other students).

There are no discussion boards in this course, but a portion of your Team Assignments grade will come from your participation on Slack. In addition, if you have a pressing question about something, you can often get a response faster by sending a direct message to the instructor on Slack than by emailing.

Slack Etiquette

It's important to be professional in your online communications. Aside from the standard guidelines of not using ALL-CAPS TO COMMUNICATE and speaking respectfully, you should be aware of Slack-specific etiquette.
